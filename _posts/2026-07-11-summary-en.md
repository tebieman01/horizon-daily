## [Score Accuracy Does Not Guarantee Diffusion Sampler Stability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08757) ⭐️ 9.0/10

A new paper proves that small forward-marginal error in score matching does not ensure numerical stability of reverse-time diffusion samplers, constructing counterexamples where Euler–Maruyama discretizations diverge in Wasserstein distances despite weak convergence. This challenges a fundamental assumption in diffusion models, revealing that widely used score-matching objectives may not guarantee reliable sampling, which could impact the deployment of diffusion models in safety-critical applications. The paper constructs a smooth score field with arbitrarily small forward-marginal L^2 error, yet its Euler–Maruyama discretizations converge in probability while every positive moment diverges, causing all Wasserstein distances W_p (p≥1) to diverge. A positive result shows that projecting the denoiser onto a bounded convex set containing the data support restores Wasserstein convergence.

rss · ArXiv - stat.ML · Jul 10, 04:00

**Background**: Diffusion models generate data by reversing a forward noising process, using a learned score function to guide the reverse-time sampling. Score matching trains the score function by minimizing the average error under the forward marginals, but the discretized sampler evaluates the score along its own trajectory, which can lead to numerical instability. The Wasserstein distance measures the difference between probability distributions, and its divergence indicates that the generated samples are far from the true data distribution.

rss · ArXiv - stat.ML · Jul 10, 04:00

**Background**: LLM ranking systems like Chatbot Arena use the Bradley-Terry model to estimate model quality from pairwise human preferences. Users compare anonymous model outputs and vote for the better one, and these votes are aggregated to produce a leaderboard. The Bradley-Terry model assumes that preferences are consistent and that small data changes do not drastically alter rankings.

## [Projected Energy Matching for 3D Generative Priors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07749) ⭐️ 7.0/10

The paper introduces Projected Energy Matching, a framework that uses Helmholtz Distillation to resolve rotational artifacts in energy-based generative models for 3D data, and Negative Caching for efficient sampling. This work addresses a fundamental structural conflict in energy-based 3D generation, enabling high-fidelity reconstructions with significantly reduced compute, which is crucial for applications like medical CT reconstruction. Helmholtz Distillation uses a Hutchinson trace estimator to absorb rotational noise into an auxiliary residual network, while Negative Caching reuses negative samples across micro-batches to make contrastive training tractable.

rss · ArXiv - q-bio.QM · Jul 10, 04:00

**Background**: Energy-based models (EBMs) define a probability distribution via an energy function, but training them on high-dimensional 3D data is challenging. Flow models are efficient but lack explicit likelihoods. Energy Matching combines both, but suffers from rotational artifacts in velocity fields when distilled from pre-trained flows.
